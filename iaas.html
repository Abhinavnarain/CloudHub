Introduction to Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS)

What is IaaS?

Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS) is a cloud computing model that provides virtualized computing resources over the internet. It enables organizations to rent virtualized hardware resources such as servers, storage, and networking, eliminating the need for physical hardware investments and allowing for scalability and flexibility.

Theoretical Concepts

IaaS offers a range of theoretical and practical benefits including:

  • Virtualization: Creates virtual instances of physical hardware to improve efficiency and resource utilization.
  • On-Demand Resources: Allows users to access and manage resources as needed without maintaining physical hardware.
  • Scalability: Easily scales resources up or down based on demand.
  • Cost Efficiency: Reduces capital expenditure by shifting to a pay-as-you-go model.

Usage of IaaS

IaaS is used for various purposes including:

  • Hosting Websites: Provides the infrastructure to host and manage websites and web applications.
  • Data Storage: Offers scalable storage solutions for large volumes of data.
  • Development and Testing: Enables developers to create and test applications without worrying about infrastructure.
  • Disaster Recovery: Provides backup and recovery solutions to ensure business continuity.
